<p><p>The intraosseous infusion devices market is categorized into two main types: manual and automatic devices. Manual intraosseous infusion devices require healthcare professionals to physically insert a needle into the bone for fluid delivery, offering simplicity and cost-effectiveness. In contrast, automatic intraosseous infusion devices utilize mechanized systems for rapid insertion and infusion, enhancing precision and reducing the time needed for access. Both types are critical in emergency medical situations where intravenous access is challenging or impossible.</p></p>
