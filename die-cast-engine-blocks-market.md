High pressure die casting involves injecting molten metal into a mold at high speeds and pressures, resulting in strong, precisely shaped components ideal for complex designs. In contrast, low pressure die casting uses lower pressures to fill the mold, producing lighter and thinner parts with superior surface finishes. Each method caters to different automotive needs and manufacturing requirements.</p></p>
