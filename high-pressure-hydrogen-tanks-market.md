<p><p>High-pressure hydrogen tanks are categorized into four types based on construction materials and design. </p><p>Type I tanks are all-metal and offer high strength but are heavy. Type II tanks combine metal with a composite overwrap for reduced weight and improved efficiency. Type III tanks use a plastic liner with a composite shell, balancing weight and pressure resistance. Type IV tanks are fully composite with a plastic liner, providing the lightest option with the best performance, ideal for various applications in hydrogen storage and transportation.</p></p>
